How Many Traders Are Profitable on Polymarket

By Andrey Sergeenkov · April 2, 2026
TL;DR
  • 15.9% of Polymarket traders are in profit, but only 2% have made more than $1,000
  • Only 6,600 addresses average more than $5,000/month, and 73% of them were active for two months or less
  • In any single month, 0.98% earned above $5,000. Two months in a row drops to 0.1%. Three months: 0.03%
  • Only 172 addresses out of 6,600 with avg profit above $5K/month were active for more than a year

If you look at the rough ratio of winners to losers, 15.9% of Polymarket traders are in profit as of the end of March 2026.

But…

Only 2% of 2.5 million traders have made more than $1,000 over their entire trading history. More than $10,000 was earned by 0.32%, about 8,000 addresses. More than $100,000 was earned by about 840 addresses, or 0.033% of all traders. About 120 addresses crossed the one million dollar mark.

What Is the Average Monthly Income on Polymarket?

1.25% of traders have an average monthly profit above $1,000. Above $5,000 is 0.26%, about 6,600 addresses. Above $10,000 per month is roughly 3,250 addresses, or 0.13% of all traders.

But How Long Do These Profitable Traders Actually Stay Active?

But the monthly profit figures above are inflated by short-term activity. More than half the addresses in each threshold were active for exactly one month. For the $1K threshold that’s 53%, for $5K also 53%, and for $10K it’s 52%.

If you combine one and two months of activity, the number is the same across all three thresholds at 73%. The consistency of this proportion shows that traders behave the same way whether they earn $1K or $10K per month.

Most traders show up, trade for a short period, and leave. Only 172 addresses out of 6,600 with average profit above $5,000 were active for more than a year. That’s 2.6%.

What if You Quit Your Job for Polymarket?

Let’s say you read this article about how Logan Sudeith quit his job and made $100k in a month. You decide to follow his lead and want to consistently earn the average US salary of $5,000 per month trading on Polymarket. I calculated the odds of earning that amount on a regular basis.

In any single month, 0.98% managed to earn more than $5,000. Two months in a row drops to 0.1%. Three months in a row is 0.03%. Four months in a row is 0.015%.

This calculation has a limitation. Every number in the table represents addresses, not necessarily people. One trader can use multiple wallets, and the real number of people consistently earning on Polymarket could be higher (because one person might switch wallets mid-period, and their six-month streak looks like two addresses with three-month streaks) or lower (because one person could trade from multiple wallets at the same time, and each one gets counted separately).

Polymarket has very different types of participants. 80% of addresses average fewer than 10 trades per active day. About 4% make more than 50 trades per day, which is nearly impossible for a human doing it manually. All of these participants with different strategies and tools compete in the same markets. Among them are bots, market makers, and potential insiders.

My Take

Trading Polymarket regularly as a source of income is extremely risky. But occasional sniper trades on markets where you have an information edge, say because you understand a particular topic better and can see the market has mispriced the probability, can produce positive results.

Methodology

The only other public study on Polymarket trader profitability that I found was done by blockchain analyst DeFi Oasis in December 2025. His data showed 30% of 1.7 million addresses in profit. I got 16%. Both formulas calculate realized PnL as the difference between incoming and outgoing USDC flows. But I include splits and merges, which DeFi Oasis apparently did not account for. When splits are left out, an address looks more profitable because one category of expenses is simply invisible. On top of that, the number of addresses grew from 1.7 to 2.5 million in the three months between the two studies, and the bulk of new accounts are small traders, most of whom lose money.

All data comes from on-chain transactions on the Polygon blockchain via Dune Analytics. Polymarket uses a hybrid architecture: orders are matched off-chain through a CLOB, but every trade settles on-chain through the CTF Exchange and NegRisk CTF Exchange smart contracts.

PnL for each address is calculated as the sum of all USDC flows across five types of on-chain events: buys and sells of outcome tokens on both exchange contracts (OrderFilled), redemption of winning tokens (PayoutRedemption), splitting USDC into YES/NO token pairs (PositionsSplit), and merging them back (PositionsMerge). Ten system addresses were excluded: three Polymarket contracts and seven infrastructure wallets from DeFi Oasis’s list.

In early iterations I calculated PnL without splits and merges. That gave 10,800 addresses with monthly profit above $5,000. After adding splits and merges the number dropped to 6,600, because splits subtract USDC from a trader’s balance.

Limitations. I only count realized PnL. If a trader bought tokens on an unresolved market and hasn’t sold them, my data shows that as a loss even though the funds are simply locked. I verified that 96% of trading volume comes from already resolved markets, and the unrealized balance on resolved markets (traders who forgot to redeem winning tokens) is about $43 million, which is minimal relative to total volume. All calculations use data through April 1, 2026.
