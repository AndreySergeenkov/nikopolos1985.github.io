Burning money — Variant 1 grid + sine waves

Heat values stored in a grid; each frame the cells average heat from below and cool a little. Bottom row seeded by layered sine waves so flame tongues rise to different heights.

Burning money — Variant 2 Doom-style propagation

Classic 1995 PSX-Doom fire algorithm. Each cell pulls its value directly from one specific cell below (with a random horizontal shift and small decay), instead of averaging neighbours. Produces coherent vertical "tongues" that look much more like real flames.

Burning money — Variant 3 rising embers

No grid at all — every flame char is an independent particle. Embers are born at the base, drift upward with a slight sideways sway, and age from yellow through orange to dark red before disappearing. Closer to a real flickering bed of coals.
